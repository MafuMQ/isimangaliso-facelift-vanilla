About Isimangaliso Wetland Park

South Africa's first World Heritage Site

Our Heritage

Isimangaliso Wetland Park, meaning "miracle" in isiZulu, was proclaimed South Africa's first World Heritage Site in 1999. This remarkable 332,000-hectare protected area stretches along 220 kilometers of pristine coastline, from the Mozambique border to Mapelane near St Lucia.

The park encompasses a mosaic of pristine marine reserves, beaches, coral reefs, lakes, swamps, grasslands and coastal forests that are home to one of the largest concentrations of hippos and Nile crocodiles in Africa, as well as 526 bird species.

World Heritage Status

Isimangaliso meets all four natural World Heritage criteria, making it one of only 39 sites globally to achieve this distinction. The park protects critical breeding and feeding grounds for sea turtles, whales, dolphins and countless other marine species.

Our History

1895

Early Protection

The first conservation measures were implemented to protect the area's unique wildlife and coastal ecosystems.

1999

World Heritage Status

Isimangaliso became South Africa's first World Heritage Site, recognized for its outstanding universal value.

2000

Wetland Park Authority

The Isimangaliso Wetland Park Authority was established to manage and protect this precious ecosystem.

2024

Continued Excellence

Today, we continue to lead in conservation, research, and sustainable tourism practices.
